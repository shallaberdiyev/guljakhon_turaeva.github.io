About

I am a Master's researcher in Area Studies at the School of Foreign Languages, Shenzhen University. My research centers on media discourse, international communication, BRI media framing, and socio-political analysis of Central Asia, with a particular focus on Uzbekistan's representation in global media.

Prior to my MA, I served as an Assistant to the Dean of the Faculty of Foreign Philology at Termez State University and as a teacher of English technical terminology at secondary schools in Tashkent. I hold a BA in English and German Philology (Translation Theory and Practice) from the National University of Uzbekistan (CGPA 3.60/4.0).

I am a recipient of the El-Yurt Umidi Foundation Scholarship (2023) and the Shenzhen Universiade International Scholarship (2025-2026).

Research Interests

  • Media discourse analysis - framing, representation, and narrative construction
  • BRI (Belt and Road Initiative) media framing and Uzbekistan coverage in international press
  • International communication and global governance
  • Socio-political analysis of Central Asian region
  • Area studies methodology - comparative and interdisciplinary approaches
  • Corpus linguistics and computational approaches to discourse analysis
  • Child language acquisition and applied linguistics
  • English for technology and technical terminology in education
